🔍 Essential Morocco Tips — Read Before You Go
🏪 Souk Survival
💬
Always negotiate. Starting price in souks is 3–5× the real price. Start at 30% of their ask, meet in the middle. Never accept first price.
🚶
Ignore "guides". Anyone who approaches offering to show you around the medina will ask for money at the end. Say "la shukran" (no thank you).
📸
Photos = money. Snake charmers, monkey handlers, henna ladies — touching or photographing means you owe them. Be decisive.
🍽️ Food Rules
🍺
No alcohol in the medina. Most medina restaurants don't serve alcohol. Hotels and bars in Gueliz (modern district) do. Don't expect it at traditional riads.
🌿
Mint tea is never free. Accepting tea at any shop = you're expected to buy something. If you just want tea, sit at a café and order it (15–20 MAD).
💧
Bottled water only. Don't drink tap water or ice in restaurants. Buy 1.5L bottles at any épicerie (~5 MAD). Stay hydrated — March is warm.
👗 Dress Code
👔
Cover up in the medina. Shoulders + knees covered for both men and women. Light linen works — it's warm but modest. Locals will respect you more.
🚕 Getting Around
🚖
Always agree price BEFORE getting in petit taxi. They don't use meters reliably. Medina to Majorelle = ~30 MAD. Airport to medina = ~70–100 MAD. Have dirhams ready.
🗺️
Download Maps.me offline. Google Maps works but offline medina maps on Maps.me are better for the narrow alleyways (derbs) — streets change and GPS drifts.

🕐Arrival Plan
Arrive RAK Airport
🛬 Marrakech Menara Airport (RAK) — 5km from medina
Taxi to riad: agree price BEFORE getting in — ~70–100 MAD. Say "medina" + riad name. Journey: ~15min. Don't use unlicensed drivers at arrivals.
📍 RAK Airport
Check in
🏨 Riad check-in — riads often have unmarked doors in narrow alleys
Don't panic if you can't find it — call the riad, they'll send someone to collect you from a landmark. This is normal. WhatsApp works.
Evening
🚶 Walk to Jemaa el-Fna — ~10min from any medina riad
Largest open square in Africa. At night it transforms: storytellers, musicians, acrobats, smoke from food stalls. This is the heart of Marrakech. Just walk in and absorb it.
📍 Jemaa el-Fna
Dinner — Jemaa el-Fna food stalls
🍢 Sit at the numbered food stalls in the square
Stalls 1–100 are permanent evening stalls. Point at what you want. Order: merguez sausages, brochettes (skewers), harira soup (thick tomato + lentil, ~10 MAD/bowl), sheep head (adventurous). Budget ~80–120 MAD total. Vendors will grab you — just sit at any stall and they'll bring menus.
📍 Food Stalls
Night tip
🍵 Mint tea on a rooftop café overlooking Jemaa el-Fna
Café de France or Café Glacier (rooftop terrace) — best view of the whole square. Mint tea ~20–30 MAD. Order it "sans sucre" if you don't want it overly sweet. The view alone is worth it.
📍 Café de France
🔍 First Night Survival Guide
🐍 What You'll See at Jemaa el-Fna
🐍
Snake charmers: If you photograph or get close, they'll demand 50–100 MAD. Watch from a distance or just walk past confidently.
🐒
Monkey handlers: Same deal — touching = paying. Photos = paying. Don't make eye contact if you don't want to engage.
💃
Henna ladies: They'll grab your hand and start drawing. If you don't want it, say "la" (no) firmly and keep walking.
✅
What's genuinely free: The Gnaoua musicians, storytellers, acrobats — these perform for the crowd, no obligation. Tip if you enjoy (5–10 MAD).
💸 Cash Tips
🏧
Get dirhams at airport ATM. Wafa Bank or CIH at arrivals give good rates. Withdraw 2,000–3,000 MAD for first day. Most medina shops and restaurants are cash only.
📵
Dirhams cannot be taken out of Morocco. Don't withdraw too much — use it all up before departure or convert back at airport (poor rate).
Marrakech — Full Day
Fri Mar 20 · Souks · Bahia Palace · Majorelle Garden · Night Market
🕐Full Day Plan
07:30
☕ Riad breakfast — included in most riads
Traditional spread: msemen (flaky flatbread), honey, amlou (almond-argan butter paste), fresh-squeezed OJ. Eat well — busy day ahead.
09:00 — Souks
🛍️ Enter the souks from Jemaa el-Fna (north end)
Each souk sells one category: Souk Semmarine (carpets + leather), Souk des Babouches (leather slippers), Souk des Teinturiers (dyers souk, vivid hanging fabric), Souk Spice Square (ras el hanout, saffron). Get lost — that's the point. Allow 2hrs.
📍 Souks Entrance📍 Dyers' Souk
09:30 — Buy Here
🌿 Spice Square (Place des Épices)
Buy: Saffron (~50 MAD/gram, genuine), Ras el Hanout spice blend (~40 MAD), Rose water (~30 MAD). Don't buy "Berber Viagra" or mystery powders they push on tourists. Negotiate everything — start at 40% of asking price.
📍 Spice Square
11:30 — Must See
🏛️ Ben Youssef Madrasa — most beautiful building in Marrakech
Former Islamic school founded 1565. Stunning carved stucco, zellige tile, cedar wood. Entrance ~70 MAD. Allow 45min. Students once lived in the tiny rooms around the courtyard — look for the carved inscriptions.
📍 Ben Youssef
13:00 — Lunch
🍲 Nomad Restaurant — rooftop, modern Moroccan
Above the spice market. Vegetable tagine or chicken bastilla (~120–150 MAD). Book ahead or arrive early. Alternatively: Café Clock near Riad Zitoun for traditional msemen + harira (60 MAD total). Both are tourist-friendly but genuine.
📍 Nomad📍 Café Clock
14:30
🏰 Bahia Palace — 19th century vizier's palace
Entry ~70 MAD. The building was designed to be the greatest palace of its time — 8 hectares of gardens and ornate rooms. The vizier had 4 wives and 24 concubines, each with their own suite. The French later used it as a government building during colonisation. Allow 45min.
📍 Bahia Palace
16:00
🌿 Majorelle Garden + YSL Museum PRE-BOOK
Taxi from palace: ~25 MAD. Blue-painted garden designed by French painter Majorelle, saved from destruction and restored by Yves Saint Laurent. Cobalt blue buildings + cacti + fountains. Entry: garden ~150 MAD, YSL museum extra 100 MAD. Instagram heaven but genuinely beautiful. YSL is buried in the garden. Pre-book online — queues are long.
📍 Majorelle Garden🌿 Book →
18:30
🚖 Taxi back to medina · Freshen up at riad
19:30 — PEAK TIME
🎭 Jemaa el-Fna at its best — sunset and after dark
The square is completely different at 19:30 vs daytime. All the food stalls are lit up, musicians are playing, storytellers have crowds. Walk the full perimeter first, then pick a food stall. Order: merguez, snail soup (yes, really — 10 MAD a bowl, a local classic), grilled fish, fresh orange juice from the stalls (~4 MAD/glass — best OJ you'll have).
📍 Jemaa el-Fna
21:00 — Dinner
🍽️ Le Jardin — garden restaurant, Moroccan classics
Hidden in the medina (Mouassine area). Orange trees overhead, lanterns everywhere. Order: chicken pastilla (sweet-savoury pie with almonds + spices, ~120 MAD), lamb tagine with prunes (~110 MAD), kefta mechoui. Reservation recommended — WhatsApp +212 5243-78295. ~300–400 MAD pp for full meal.
📍 Le Jardin
🔍 Souk Buying Guide — What's Worth It
✅ Worth Buying
🌹
Argan oil — food-grade (culinary) or cosmetic. Buy from a women's argan cooperative, not souk vendors. Authentic: ~150–200 MAD/250ml. Souk stuff is often diluted.
🧦
Babouche slippers — leather, hand-stitched. ~80–150 MAD if you negotiate. Yellow = traditional men's, pointed = decorative. Souk des Babouches for best selection.
🪔
Brass lanterns — authentic hand-punched brass, not cheap cast versions. Test by tapping: real brass rings, fake thuds. ~150–400 MAD depending on size.
🌿
Saffron — Moroccan saffron is world-class. Buy stigma threads (not powder = adulterated). ~50 MAD/gram at spice square. Half of what you'd pay in Europe.
❌ Skip These
🪨
Fossils + "argan" products at souks — often fake. The roadside fossil sellers near Ourika sell the real thing at better prices.
🧴
Souk argan oil — usually adulterated. Buy from a certified women's cooperative instead.
🍽️Must Eat Today
🥧
Bastilla (Pastilla) — sweet-savoury pigeon/chicken pie in filo pastry with almonds + cinnamon. Most unique dish in Morocco. ~120 MAD
🍲
Lamb tagine with prunes — slow-cooked in a clay cone, sweet + rich. ~110 MAD
🥣
Harira soup — thick tomato + lentil + chickpea soup. 10–15 MAD/bowl. Order at food stalls.
🍊
Fresh-squeezed orange juice — Jemaa el-Fna stalls. ~4 MAD/glass. Best you'll ever have. Order 3.
🐌
Snail soup (babbouche) — tiny snails in herbal broth, 10 MAD/bowl. Optional but very local.

🕐Day Plan
08:00
🚗 Driver pickup from riad · Head south on Route d'Ourika
45min drive to valley. Ask driver to stop at the roadside argan oil cooperative (women-run, about 20min from Marrakech) — sample and buy authentic argan oil here at fair prices.
📍 Ourika Valley
09:30 — Berber Villages
🏘️ Ourika Valley — Berber village stops
Tell your driver: "stop at authentic Berber village, not tourist stop." The valley has real working villages with terraced fields. Watch: pottery making, weaving, traditional bread baking. Don't feel obligated to buy — but small purchases (10–20 MAD for bread, small pottery) are appreciated.
10:30 — Waterfall Hike
💧 Setti Fatma Waterfalls — 7 cascading falls
Entry to hike: ~10 MAD. Takes 45min–1hr to reach all 7 (you don't have to do all). First waterfall is easy (10min), the others require scrambling. Wear decent shoes — the path is slippery. Local boys will try to "guide" you — you don't need them for the first falls. ~100 MAD if you take a guide for the full set (negotiable).
📍 Setti Fatma Falls
12:30 — Lunch
🍲 Berber village restaurant in the valley
Roadside restaurants in Ourika serve the best tagine of the trip. Sit on the terrace above the river. Order: tagine of lamb or chicken (~80 MAD), fresh bread (~10 MAD), mint tea. Don't overpay — 80–120 MAD is right for a full lunch.
14:30
🐪 Atlas viewpoint + optional camel ride
The views of the High Atlas peaks (some snow-capped in March) are stunning. Camel rides at tourist stops: ~100–150 MAD for 20min. Negotiate hard. It's a bit touristy but it's the moment. Skip if you've done it before.
16:00
🚗 Drive back to Marrakech
18:00 — Hammam
🛁 Traditional Hammam experience — Les Bains de Marrakech
Book in advance: lesbainsdemarrakech.com. Classic hammam + kessa scrub + rhassoul clay mask = ~300–500 MAD pp. The kessa scrub removes layers of dead skin — you won't believe what comes off. Book the "Traditional Hammam" not the fancy spa version. Allow 1.5hrs. Bring nothing — they provide everything.
📍 Les Bains🛁 Book →
20:30 — Dinner
🍽️ Dinner in the Mouassine neighbourhood
After hammam you'll be relaxed and hungry. Moroccan salad spread (zaalouk + taktouka + hummus) + couscous (Fridays are the traditional couscous day in Morocco). Restaurant Dar Zitoun is excellent for traditional dinner. ~150–200 MAD pp.
📍 Mouassine
🔍 Atlas Mountains Local Intel
🌡️ Weather in March
🧥
Bring a jacket. The valley is noticeably cooler than Marrakech (can be 10–15°C at higher elevations). Light layer needed especially for waterfalls hike.
❄️
Snow on peaks. In March the High Atlas peaks above 2,500m are often snow-capped — spectacular backdrop. If you want to actually be in snow, ask driver to go toward Oukaïmeden ski resort (~1.5hrs from Marrakech, 2,650m).
🛒 What to Buy Here
🧴
Authentic argan oil — from the women's cooperative on the way up. Food grade (~200 MAD/250ml) or cosmetic (~150 MAD). Far better quality than souks.
🪨
Fossils — Morocco is one of the world's top fossil sites. Ammonites and trilobites from local roadside sellers are usually real (Morocco exports millions). ~30–80 MAD for a nice piece. Negotiate.

🕐Essaouira Day Plan
07:30
☕ Quick riad breakfast · Head to bus/taxi station by 08:00
~10:30 — Arrive
🌊 Arrive Essaouira · Walk to the port first
Essaouira is completely different from Marrakech — cool ocean breeze, white-and-blue buildings, totally relaxed. No hassling, no aggressive vendors. The contrast will hit you immediately.
📍 Essaouira Port
11:00 — MUST DO
🐟 Fresh seafood at the port fish stalls
Walk along the port wall — rows of blue fishing boats + fish stalls. Choose your fish fresh from the display, they grill it right there. ~60–80 MAD for sardines + grilled prawns + bread. The freshest fish of your life. Haggle slightly. Eat standing up with locals. This is THE Essaouira experience.
📍 Fish Stalls
12:30
🏰 Ramparts walk (Skala de la Ville)
Free. Walk along the Portuguese-built sea ramparts — massive cannons still pointing out to sea, ocean crashing below. The Atlantic views are spectacular. Essaouira was a major Portuguese trading port — this is why the architecture looks different from Marrakech. Allow 30min.
📍 Ramparts
13:30
🎵 Gnaoua music in the medina
Essaouira is the birthplace of Gnaoua music (African spiritual music tradition). You'll hear it everywhere. The medina here is calm — browse silver jewellery, thuya wood crafts (local specialty), goat leather bags without being harassed.
📍 Essaouira Medina
14:30
🛍️ Buy: Thuya Wood + Silver
Essaouira's specialties: thuya wood (aromatic local wood carved into boxes, chessboards, frames — unique to this region, ~80–200 MAD), silver Berber jewellery (more authentic here than Marrakech), argan oil products (coastal cooperatives). Prices lower and quality higher than Marrakech souks.
15:30
🌊 Essaouira beach — kite surfing capital of Africa
Massive wide beach south of the medina. Strong Atlantic wind makes it perfect for kite surfing — dozens of kites always in the air. Walk the beach for 20min (it's windy — fun). Don't go too far as sandstorms can be intense. Watch the kite surfers from the beach wall.
📍 Essaouira Beach
16:00
🚌 Head back to bus/taxi station for return to Marrakech
Don't cut it too close — last CTM bus is usually 18:00. Arrive at station 30min early. Check the time on arrival.
~19:00
Arrive Marrakech · Dinner in medina
Simple final evening: rooftop restaurant in the medina. Al Fassia (female-run, all-female staff, best traditional Moroccan in Marrakech, ~250 MAD pp) or Riad Yima (Andy Warhol-meets-Morocco décor, quirky, ~150 MAD pp).
📍 Al Fassia
🔍 Essaouira Local Intel
🎬 Famous Connections
🎸
Jimi Hendrix — spent time here in 1969. There's a Jimi Hendrix café near the medina. He supposedly wrote "Castles Made of Sand" here (disputed, but the legend stuck).
👑
Game of Thrones — Astapor (the Unsullied slave city) was filmed in Essaouira. The port/rampart area is instantly recognisable if you're a fan.
🎭
Orson Welles' Othello — filmed here in 1952. The ramparts and square are in the opening scenes. There's a plaque near the port.
🌬️ Weather Warning
💨
Wind is constant and strong. Essaouira is literally called "the windy city." Bring a light scarf/jacket even in March. The beach can have sandstorms. Your photos will have windswept hair. Embrace it.
Last Morning + Fly Home
Mon Mar 23 · Saadian Tombs · Last souk · ✈️ Depart
🧳 Full checkout riad. Bags to airport. Store with riad if you want last morning free.
🕐Day Plan
07:30
☕ Final riad breakfast · Checkout or store bags
Most riads will store your bags until your taxi — ask at reception.
09:00
⚰️ Saadian Tombs — discovered hidden for 200 years
Entry ~70 MAD. The 16th century tombs of the Saadian dynasty were walled up by a rival sultan and forgotten. Rediscovered in 1917 by French colonial aerial photographs. Stunning carved stucco and Italian Carrara marble. The "Chamber of 12 Pillars" is one of the finest examples of Moorish architecture in Morocco. Only 2 rooms visible but worth every dirham. Allow 30min — gets crowded after 10am so go early.
📍 Saadian Tombs
10:00
🏚️ El Badi Palace ruins
Entry ~70 MAD. What's left of what was once the most magnificent palace in the world (16th century). Stripped of all marble, gold, and ornament by a rival sultan. What remains: a vast empty courtyard, sunken gardens, stork nests on every tower, underground passages. It's hauntingly beautiful in its ruin. Allow 30min.
📍 El Badi Palace
11:00
🛍️ Last souk run — anything you missed
Spend remaining dirhams. Remember: dirhams cannot leave Morocco (you can convert at airport but rate is terrible). Use them up. Good last buys: saffron, argan oil, babouche slippers, small brass teapot.
12:30
🍲 Final lunch — Café Arabe or Terrasse des Épices
Café Arabe: rooftop, great views of medina. Order: couscous royale (huge) or simple tagine. Terrasse des Épices: above spice market, excellent for final views. ~100–150 MAD pp.
📍 Café Arabe
Airport
🚖 Taxi to RAK Airport — agree price first: ~70–100 MAD
Allow 30min journey + 2hrs before international flight. RAK airport is small — you'll be through security in 20min but go early. Duty free: buy extra argan oil and Moroccan spice sets at airport (accepts euros/cards, reasonable prices).
📍 RAK Airport
🔍 Airport + Final Tips
✈️ Airport Tips
💸
Spend all dirhams before airport. The airport conversion rate is terrible. Buy last-minute gifts, tip the riad staff (50–100 MAD per person per night is standard), use dirhams for taxi.
🧴
Liquids in hand luggage. If you bought argan oil, move to checked baggage. Airport security is strict about liquids.
🛍️
Duty free is worth it. RAK airport has good argan oil + spice sets at fair prices and accepts cards. Better option than converting leftover cash.
🙏 Riad Tipping
💰
Standard: 50–100 MAD per person per night for the main staff (housekeeper, breakfast person). Leave at reception or hand directly. It makes a huge difference to their income.
